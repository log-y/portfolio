What I've Been Working On

Undergraduate Researcher at Tufts University

Built an ecosystem for developing and distributing audio transcription plugins. (Fall 2024)

GailBot is an audio transcription tool that accounts for the paralinguistic features of audio (like voice intonation, prosody, etc). The API software was an award-winning breakthrough in human interaction, winning the 2023 Sage Concepts Grant Award ($20,000) and being published in a prestigious journal, Dialogue and Discourse. My team and I are building an ecosystem around GailBot, allowing other developers to build and share their own machine learning plugins. We built a new website for uploads and downloads of these plugins using React and AWS. We are also working on a Python package (published to PyPI) where people can access developer tools to continue building their plugins.

Summer Research Assistant at the University of Houston

Used a number of deep learning methods to analyze processes in fluid dynamics. (Summer 2024)

When people design any product that involves running fluids through a porous surface (like air filters, athletic wear, or a sprinkler), they track factors that will dictate how the fluid flows (like surface area, roughness, waviness). One particular measure is 'tortuosity', a measure of the 'complexity' of a fluid's path. My goal was to use a university dataset (a collection of labeled images) to help predict that tortuosity. I mainly used a convolutional neural network (using PyTorch), but I also used lighter models, like linear regression models and decision trees. A fun introduction to the field! Shoutout to my mentor, endowed professor Qin Guan.
